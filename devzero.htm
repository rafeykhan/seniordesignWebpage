IEEE Robotics Competition 2017 (Denver, CO)

Overview

Mission

To design and build a competition robot that demonstrates our collective skills gained over our college careers.

Vision

Build a competition robot that IEEE has never seen before.

Success Criteria

To create a functioning autonomous robot within our given time place which enables our team to place in the competiton.

Competition:

Competition Date: April 1st, 2017

Motivation:
The objective was to use an autonomous robot on the surface to map a tunnel and investigate “buried caches” at the tunnel endpoint.

The game field is meant to simulate a tunnel containing infrastructure such as steel cables or rails for material transport and electrical conductors for lighting or communications. Surface obstacles and anomalies represent typical operational challenge.

Objectives:
Key objectives for teams of the competition include mapping the field and its tunnel, and reading and displaying a value on a die in a container at the end of the main tunnel.

/DEV/ZERO

Senior Design Project

Abdul R. Khan, De Do, Jarrod Nix, Jake Skodak, Greg Stout

Design

The /dev/zero competition robot is composed of five major system layers:
Artificial Intelligence, Manipulation, Perception, Drive and User Interaction. All of these layers reside within a foundation layer of power management. The Artificial Intelligence is connected to each of the other system layers, through which data is distributed.

Results

1. Placed 5th (top 5) out of 32 teams

2. Completed robot within given 8 months time frame

3. Built replica of game field used at competition

4. Only team to attempt to read die in container at end of main tunnel

5. Successfully implemented “crab-drive” system using 3D printer parts and concepts

6. Near fully custom fabricated design implementation
